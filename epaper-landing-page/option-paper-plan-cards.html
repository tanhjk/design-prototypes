Chapter one

Somewhere along the way, reading the news got loud.

Endless feeds, pop-ups, the next thing and the next. E-paper steps back to something steadier: a real newspaper layout you can hold in one hand. The same page that printed, the same order the editors intended — only lighter, and always with you.

Chapter two · What you gain

Your place to read.

Everything that made print feel right, kept intact — and a few quiet things it never could do.

01 — Printed view

The page, exactly as printed.

Issues keep their true newspaper layout — masthead, lead story and photographs where the editor placed them. The page you see is the page that printed.

02 — Curated & structured

The editor's order, kept whole.

Organised by section and editorial priority, not by an algorithm — so you read with intent, and reach the end of a section instead of an endless feed.

03 — Offline reading

Yours, with no signal.

Download an issue and read anywhere — the train, the plane, the quiet cabin off-grid. The paper never asks for a connection.

04 — Sharing

Worth passing along.

Share a page in a tap, the way you'd slide the paper across the table. The same faithful layout opens on the other side.
